# Code Of Conduct

## Purpose

This project should remain a respectful academic and technical workspace. Contributions, reviews, and discussions should help improve the work without undermining the people involved.

## Expected Behaviour

- Be respectful, clear, and constructive.
- Give feedback on the work, not personal attributes.
- Credit data sources, academic support, and external references accurately.
- Raise concerns early when something may affect reproducibility, integrity, or assessment fairness.
- Keep private information, credentials, and unpublished assessment material out of public channels unless sharing is explicitly allowed.

## Unacceptable Behaviour

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Plagiarism, misrepresentation of authorship, or hiding material assistance.
- Uploading secrets, personal data, or restricted files without permission.
- Intentionally weakening validation, security checks, or documentation to make results look better.

## Reporting

For coursework-related matters, follow Singapore Polytechnic and module reporting channels. For repository maintenance matters, open an issue or contact the repository owner with:

- what happened;
- where it happened;
- the impact;
- any relevant links, screenshots, or files.

Reports should be handled fairly, privately where appropriate, and with attention to academic integrity requirements.
